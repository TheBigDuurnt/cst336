A Brief History of Malware.

Early forms of malware often spread to computers through floppy disks that were traded between people. The first type of virus known was known as the “Creeper Worm” , created by BBN Technologies’ Bob Thomas, who was experimenting with self-replicating programs. These worms would display a message that said “I’m the creeper, catch me if you can!”

Early forms of malware were often harmless and benign, but over the course of years, these messages evolved into a more sinister industry. In the 2000s, these viruses had become more malicious, and spread much quicker. SQL injections became a large potential problem, and corporations, e.g. IKEA, had become victims to these malicious attacks.
